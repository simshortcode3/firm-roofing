Protect Your Investment: Rainy Season Roof Maintenance Guide

5 Must-Do Tasks Every Homeowner Should Perform Before the Rains Arrive

Why Pre-Rain Maintenance Is Non-Negotiable

Kenya’s rainy seasons can be intense—overflowing gutters, storm damage, and roof leaks can compromise your home and safety. But with just a few essential checks, you can prevent thousands of shillings in damage and protect your investment long term.

1. Clean Gutters & Downspouts

Remove debris, leaves, and nests to prevent water from backing up and damaging your fascia or interior ceilings.

2. Inspect for Loose or Damaged Roofing

Check for cracked tiles, rusty sheets, or unsecured ridges—repairing early prevents full-blown leaks.

3. Seal Flashings & Roof Penetrations

Use roofing sealant around chimneys, skylights, and vent pipes to prevent water seepage.

4. Improve Attic Insulation

Ensure insulation is dry and intact. Wet insulation weakens your roof and affects indoor comfort.

5. Schedule a Professional Roof Checkup

Spot the Warning Signs Early

Ceiling Stains

Discoloration often signals roof leakage—especially around light fixtures and corners.

Clogged Gutters

If water isn’t flowing, it's time to clear out that mess before the floods come.
