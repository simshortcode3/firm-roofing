Material Roofing in Kenya: Benefits and Installation Tips

Choosing the Right Roof Material for Performance, Longevity & Style

Why Material Matters in Roofing

Your roofing material defines not just the look of your home, but its resilience, energy efficiency, and long-term cost. With Kenya's diverse climate—from humid coastlines to highland rains—it’s crucial to pick the right material and install it correctly.

1. Galvanized Steel

Benefits: Affordable, corrosion-resistant, lightweight
Best for: Coastal & rainy areas

2. Clay Tiles

Benefits: Fireproof, insulating, long-lasting
Best for: Urban & dry zones

3. Concrete Tiles

Benefits: Durable, aesthetic, eco-friendly
Best for: Highlands & modern homes

Installation Tips for Each Material

Galvanized Steel

Ensure a moisture barrier and proper screw fasteners. Leave room for expansion in hot zones.

Clay Tiles

Use strong timber support. Apply waterproof underlayment. Tiles should interlock securely.

Concrete Tiles

Reinforce rafters for weight. Seal ridges well to avoid leakage. Ideal slope: 30°+

Expert Tips

  • Always check for SABS or KEBS certification when buying tiles.
  • Use breathable roofing felt to avoid trapped moisture.
  • Consider color-coated metal sheets for heat reflection.
